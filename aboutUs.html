    About Signature Overview

    Signature Bank is India's largest private sector bank with total consolidated assets of Rs. 11,242.81 billion (US$ 172.5 billion) at March 31, 2018 and profit after tax of Rs. 67.77 billion (US$ 1.0 billion) for the year ended March 31, 2018. Signature Bank currently has a network of 4,867 Branches and 14,367 ATMs across India.

    History

    Signature Bank was originally promoted in 1994 by Signature Limited, an Indian financial institution, and was its wholly-owned subsidiary.

    In the 1990s, Signature transformed its business from a development financial institution offering only project finance to a diversified financial services group offering a wide variety of products and services, both directly and through a number of subsidiaries and affiliates like Signature Bank. In 1999, Signature become the first Indian company and the first bank or financial institution from non-Japan Asia to be listed on the NYSE.

    In October 2001, the Boards of Directors of Signature and Signature Bank approved the merger of Signature and two of its wholly-owned retail finance subsidiaries, Signature Personal Financial Services Limited and Signature Capital Services Limited, with Signature Bank.
